中国二十四节气——立春与冬至

The 24 Solar Terms of China —— Start of Spring and Winter Solstice

二十四节气概述

Overview of the 24 Solar Terms

二十四节气是中国传统文化的重要组成部分，生动地反映了季节、气候和物候的变化规律。其中，立春和冬至意义非凡。

The 24 solar terms, an important part of China's traditional culture, vividly reflect the changing rules of seasons, climate, and phenology. Among them, Start of Spring and Winter Solstice hold special significance.

立春 Start of Spring

The first solar term among the 24 solar terms

立春是二十四节气中的第一个节气，标志着春季的开始。此时，天气逐渐转暖，东风解冻，蛰虫始振，万物开始复苏。

Start of Spring is the first solar term among the 24 solar terms, marking the beginning of spring. At this time, the weather gradually gets warmer. The east wind thaws the ice, hibernating insects start to stir, and all things begin to recover.

立春习俗 Customs

在立春时节，人们有迎春、咬春等习俗，迎春是为了迎接春天的到来，表达对新一年的美好期望；咬春则通常会吃春饼、萝卜等食物，寓意着迎接春天的生机与活力。

During the Start of Spring, people have customs such as welcoming spring and biting spring. Welcoming spring is to greet the arrival of spring and express good expectations for the new year. When biting spring, people usually eat spring cakes, radishes and other foods, implying the embrace of the vitality and energy of spring.

冬至 Winter Solstice

The 22nd solar term among the 24 solar terms

冬至是二十四节气中第二十二个节气，拉开寒冬的深邃序幕。这时，白昼最短、黑夜最长，此后阳光渐长。北方冷风凛冽，南方湿寒入骨，万物在严寒中潜藏。

The Winter Solstice is the 22nd solar term among the twenty-four solar terms, marking the profound beginning of the cold winter. At this time, the daytime is the shortest and the nighttime is the longest, and after that, the duration of sunlight gradually increases. In the north, the cold wind blows fiercely, while in the south, the damp cold pierces to the bone, and all things lie dormant in the severe cold.

冬至习俗 Customs

在冬至时节，人们有祭祖、吃特色食物等习俗。祭祖是为了缅怀先辈，寄托对先人的追思与敬意；北方吃饺子，避免 "冻耳朵"，南方吃汤圆，寓意团圆美满，这些习俗承载着对温暖、安康的期盼。

During the Winter Solstice, people have customs such as ancestor worship and eating special foods. Ancestor worship is to commemorate ancestors and express remembrance and respect for them. In the north, people eat dumplings to avoid "frozen ears", while in the south, people eat tangyuan, symbolizing reunion and happiness. These customs carry people's expectations for warmth and well-being.
